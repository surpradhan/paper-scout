How it works 5 stages
1
Search
Fetch up to 20 papers from arXiv sorted by relevance
arxiv_search()
2
Fast Filter
Batch score with Llama 3.1 8B — drop anything below 4.0 in one call
quick_filter()
3
Detail Score
Kimi K2 rates each paper on relevance, novelty, and clarity
llm_summarize()
4
Recency Boost
Papers published within 6 months get +1.0, within a year +0.5
recency_boost()
5
Re-rank
Relative scoring pass compares candidates against each other, returns top N
rerank()
Why not just ask Claude or GPT?
01
Zero hallucination risk
Every paper comes directly from arXiv — real titles, real authors, real abstracts. No LLM memory, no fabricated citations.
02
Tunable scoring
Weight relevance, novelty, and clarity to match your goal. Bias toward cutting-edge work or accessible reads — your call.
03
Relative re-ranking
Papers are scored against each other, not in isolation. The best paper for your query rises — not just the most popular one.
04
"Why it matters" per paper
Each result includes a query-specific explanation of why this paper is worth your time — not a generic abstract rewrite.
05
Programmatic by design
Import run() into any script. Schedule as a cron job, expose via API, or chain as a tool in a multi-agent pipeline. The UI is optional.
06
Recency-aware
Fresh papers from the last 6 months get a score boost. Recent work surfaces even when it hasn't accumulated citations yet.
